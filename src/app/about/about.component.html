About

I was born in Lebanon, a small country on the Mediterranean. I was first introduced to computers at the age of 5 when my brother bought a Pentium 2 computer. As I grew, I started to have an interest in how computers work and how they are built. I was also fascinated by the impact they had and the immense potential of software and interactive arts. In 10th grade, I started diving into robotics and electronics, took software classes and formed a background in the field. I chose to go abroad and study at Penn State because I wanted to broaden my horizons and get access to the wonderful academic and professional opportunities provided by the university. I have worked at small and big companies back home and in the States since then. I have also participated in many competitions at Penn State and worked on projects I am very proud of. Having acquired a strong background in software development, I now have five areas of interest: C++, Angular Development, UX/UI Design, Game Development and Cognitive Psychology. I am currently focused on UX/UI Design and Psychology. In the future, I aim to lead the initiative in influencing other people's lives in a positive way.
